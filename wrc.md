I observe that software and consulting industries are the two industries that adopt subscription payment the most, potentially due to having employees with technical background to setup. On the other hand,

Two strategies:
    precise marketing:
        small business vs. big business
            talk to those small businesses who adopt subscription for every industry. Try to gather information on how they do so and the benefits of adopting it. Talk to small businesses who do not adopt subscription and ask why they do not adopt it and what are the challenges. Make (1) commercial to advertise the benefits of adopting it (2) provide step-by-step documentation so people without technical background can follow along and setup it. Despite that each of the small business contribute proportionally very little. Their total volume actually exceeds the total of all big businesses, meaning that if we are able to make them adopt subscription, it will be a win-win because we grow the subscription business and deepen the relationship with (so they less likely to go with our competitors) and they reduce the time spent on processing every single recurring payment manually.

    mass promotion:
        A potential reason that a lot of merchants do not adopt subscription could simply because they do not know about it. We can promote our product by sending marketing information (potentially with some promotion) to those who did not adopt. This is to raise awareness and make them know about the existence of this product.
